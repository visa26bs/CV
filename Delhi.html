Away from Home, Part 2, Delhi




  • Delhi is a beautiful, well planned city as it rightly should be
  • I witnessed Independence day at the Rajpath
  • I was living in a working women's hostel which means living with people from all parts of the country.
  • At the hostel we celebrated north indian festivals, introduced them to south indian festivals. We also celebrated our festivals, their way
  • As happens with every other South Indian, I was also annoyed at their sheer ignorance of South India
  • I thought I will be acing my job since I knew Hindi. But everyone around me was speaking in different variants of Hindi. I can offcourse understand what they are saying but I could'nt hold an entire conversation in Hindi. Neither could I answer as fast as I would like to, in Hindi. I also realised how I was coming up with a lot of literal translations in my head while I speak.
  • I knew only some of the north indian dishes. When I had lunch with my colleagues, I discovered so many more dishes and also the hindi names of common vegetables.
  • My lunch was mostly chapathi and some side dish (hostel food). I am not used to eating chapathi with spoon, unlike North Indians. Now I am regarded as a Southie who eats with their hands. In Ariyalur, I was the culture ridden girl eating with spoon. Matlab, Hadh hai!
  • People would never know my caste, unless I told it; Since I dont have a Surname. But even though they had a surname I dint have knowledge of the caste names in North India. haha. Happily unaware. But I understood, it was very much part of social interactions.
  • I loved exploring the city all by myself
  • I loved winter shopping with my friends, from the famous shopping streets of Delhi.
  • I loved Dilli HAAT
  • I loved the spirit of Ministry meetings - the people, their dress, the language, it was beautiful.
  • I would feel very happy to find Tamil speaking folks, once in a while. I became a regular at the TAMIL SANGAM festivities. It helped to have contacts from the Sangam during Pandemic
  • There were often EXHIBITION conducted with display of items from all over the country. Exhibits included books, food, textiles etc
  • There were also exhibition of products from other countries
  • Winter fashoin was class apart
  • Christmas Eve at Connaught place looked like as if it was a European Country
  • Impromptu music bands on the streets of CP
  • Frequent strikes by civic society
  • I was already in admiration of the civic movement which elected AAP to power. But to see them fight elecion when I was in the city was too good.
  • I visited Mathura and Vrindavan over the weekend just before holi.

Matlab Dilli ki Swaad hi kuch aur hai. Achanak sa Dilli ka Sapna Adhoora reh gaya.
